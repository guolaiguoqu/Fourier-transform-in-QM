# Fourier-transform-in-QM
At the start of my second year learning quantum mechanics, I was puzzled by the different appearances of definitions of position and momentum operators in "The Physics of Quantum Mechanics" by professor Binney and professor Skinner. After a circumference with professor Burnett, who introduced another interpretation, I have been developing an interpretation using Fourier transform, which coincides with the stardard interpretation. However, I am trying to draw more explicit analogies of the interpretation with some linear algebra concepts, such as the change of basis. 
During the interpretation of the time wavefunction in TDSE, I realized that the mathematical subtleties associated with the wavefunction itself needs to be resolved, before I could discuss the particular case of time acting as a parameter.
In addition, some of my lecturenotes seem to be obscure about the normalisation when applying Fourier transforms to change the representation of wavefunctions, which motivated me to start writing down some of my thoughts.
